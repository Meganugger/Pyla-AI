May 28, 2025

Performance Investigation & A Glimpse of the Future

We revisited the performance optimizations from March, as the compiled .exe version wasn't showing the same speed boosts we saw during testing.

It turns out the "new" screenshotting library was actually a step backward. By reverting to the previous library and combining it with parallel processing, we're seeing fantastic IPS again in the development environment. Now, the focus is on ensuring these gains translate to the final build.

March 14, 2025

Major Performance Boost & Multithreading

We achieved a significant performance breakthrough!

+5-10 IPS Performance Increase

By switching to a new Python screenshotting library, we saw a raw performance increase of 12-52%, boosting IPS from ~30 to ~40 on our test systems. This change requires no extra setup for users.

Up to 48 IPS with Multi-threading

We further optimized by implementing multi-threading for screen capture. This resulted in a massive 40-78% total increase in IPS compared to our previous stable version. This will be a game-changer, especially for users with powerful GPUs where screen capture was the bottleneck.

The new build is heading to Early Access for feedback.

Breakthrough in Wall Detection Training

Huge progress on the wall detection AI! By training the model on datasets that include "fake players" (procedurally generated brawlers, health bars, and usernames), the AI is learning to identify walls even when they are partially obscured by characters.

This fixes a major issue where the bot would get stuck on walls it was standing too close to. Early training results are incredibly promising, and we're proud to say the bot can now correctly identify walls behind goalposts!

November 12-19, 2024

Training on Real Maps

Our plan to improve the wall detection model has evolved. Instead of just using randomly generated maps, we are now extracting the actual map layouts directly from the game files.

By using these real layouts combined with our 17 different background environments, we can generate a massive, high-quality dataset of over 42,000 images. Early tests on a 30k image dataset show the model is getting much better at detecting individual bushes and complex tile formations.

October 27-30, 2024

Wall Detection: Implementation & Performance

Progress

We've started implementing the new wall detection model into the bot's logic! In initial tests, the bot is already much better at avoiding shooting at walls. It can even be seen navigating around obstacles.

The Challenge

Running two AI models (one for enemies, one for walls) simultaneously is resource-intensive and effectively halves the IPS (Iterations Per Second).

The Solution

We've implemented an intelligent "caching" system. You can set the wall detection to run less frequently (e.g., 10 times per second instead of 30), and the bot will "remember" the last known wall positions. This provides a huge performance save, bringing IPS back to near-normal levels with minimal impact on accuracy.

October 6-23, 2024

The Deep Dive into Wall Detection AI

This period was a rollercoaster of intense development on our wall detection model.

The Problem

Manually labeling thousands of screenshots to train an AI is incredibly time-consuming.

The Solution

We built a custom "map maker" script that procedurally generates an almost infinite number of unique, labeled maps for training.

The Breakthrough

After many failed attempts and debugging a critical data labeling issue, we successfully trained a new YOLOv8 model. The results are phenomenal! On a small test dataset, the model is detecting walls, cacti, and barrels with high confidence.

This is the foundation for making the bot navigate maps intelligently. We are officially closer than ever to eliminating the issue of bots getting stuck on or shooting at walls.
